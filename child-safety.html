Child Safety Standards

Mohalla — Standards Against Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation (CSAE)

Last updated: 3 June 2026

Mohalla is a hyperlocal community application for neighborhood networking and rental housing. We are committed to providing a safe environment for our community and have zero tolerance for child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE) of any kind. This page sets out our standards and the measures we take to prevent, detect, and respond to child sexual abuse material (CSAM) and related conduct on our platform.

1. Zero-tolerance prohibition

The following are strictly prohibited on Mohalla and will result in immediate account termination and, where required, referral to law enforcement:

  • Any child sexual abuse material (CSAM), in any form, including images, videos, text, or links.
  • The sexualization of, sexual solicitation of, or sexual conduct involving any person under the age of 18.
  • Grooming, enticement, or any attempt to establish contact with a minor for sexual purposes.
  • Sharing, requesting, or facilitating access to CSAM.
  • Any content or behavior that endangers, exploits, or abuses children.

2. Minimum age and access

Mohalla is intended for users aged 18 and over. Accounts are created using verified phone-number authentication. We do not knowingly permit individuals under the age of 18 to register or use the service. If we become aware that a minor has created an account, we will remove the account.

3. In-app reporting

Every user can report content, messages, or other users directly within the app. Reporting tools are available on posts, chat messages, direct messages, and user profiles. Reports relating to child safety are treated as the highest priority and are reviewed promptly.

Users may also report child safety concerns directly to our dedicated point of contact listed below.

4. How we respond

  • We review reports of CSAE and act on credible reports without delay.
  • We remove violating content and disable the accounts responsible.
  • We preserve relevant information where appropriate to support investigations.
  • We report apparent CSAM to the relevant authorities, including the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) and, in India, the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (cybercrime.gov.in), in accordance with applicable law.

5. Compliance with child safety laws

Mohalla complies with all applicable child safety laws in the jurisdictions in which it operates, including obligations to report CSAM to regional and national authorities. We keep our practices under review and update them as our service and the legal landscape evolve.

6. Point of contact

We maintain a designated point of contact for child safety matters who is able to speak about our CSAM prevention and compliance practices.
